A visual arts library in the heart of South Norwood

I would love to create a library space for the community focussed on visual culture, which compliments the important contributions of Stanley Arts to the area. I envision this to be a welcoming, contemplative space for everyone—across income brackets, generations, and backgrounds—to feel a sense of place, and to be in community. I’d love to lean on the support and knowledge of Stanley Arts and local heritage groups to bringing this dream to life.

Understanding the terrain

South Norwood is home to our much-adored Brutalist library on Lawrence Rd. (saved in 2021 by local campaigning). Due to brutal cuts, it is now only open two days a week, and long-term underfunding means the range of books has stayed quite similar to when I was a child.

The second closest thing I’ve found to this project in the area is the Elizabeth James Art Gallery, which opened in 2017 and is sadly no longer in operation. (Differering from Elizabeth James Art Gallery, the space will not rely on B2C commerce as a funding stream [more information in section 5]. Despite this, I will try to get in touch with Elizabeth, as she’ll have lots of insights I could build upon.)

When the council did some research to see what kind of shops people wanted in the area, they knew exactly what they didn’t want: food shops, hairdressers, vape shops. People specifically said that they wanted art and culture.
Elizabeth James, speaking to East London Lines, 2019

Despite this being sourced from research conducted pre-pandemic, the makeup of the high street(s) remains similar. I love fast-food, and my complexion will evidence this, but I think a healthy high-street should have a mix of amenities!

Further afield, Croydon Central Library remains fantastic, but is a bus-ride away, and its underfunding is also clear. Fairfield Halls’ Arnhem Gallery is now demolished, with plans for a replacement in late 2023.

Art and design has had such a positive impact on my life, raising my horizons from a very young age. With the support, encouragement, and guidance of many art teachers (in particular, those at Croydon School of Art), I was able to enter the digital design profession, in which I have been practising for 16+ years. I would like to make accessible the things I have learned to those outside formal art education, with learning pathways into formal study and/or mentoring to enter the industry, should they wish to pursue it. For this, it will be important to create an environment in which learning can take place.

South Norwood is a deprived area with compound disadvantages. There is a very strong class current running through many art and design disciplines and I would not seek to replicate exclusionary practices, be they social or material. A key goal will be to reach, inspire and elevate those who may share similar experiences of growing up in poverty, or in migrant communities.

South Norwood has a young and diverse population. It also has a relatively high unemployment rate, and a relatively high rate of working age residents claiming out-of-work benefits.
South Norwood Community Plan, Dec 8 2018

In 2020, I co-created Islington Council's digital upskilling programme, a response to job losses and precarity due to the pandemic. For this, I provided 1-2-1 mentoring online, and helped compile free learning resources. I found the whole experience incredibly rewarding, and it made me aware how much interest there is in art and design guidance and mentoriship from those outside of the industry. I'm pleased to say we had lots of great feedback from the learners and I will source the outcomes and learnings from the course lead.

Desired location

… a largely intact former Kennedys Sausage Shop at 18 High Street provides a focal point for local organisations’ celebration of the area’s heritage.
— South Norwood Community Plan, Dec 8 2018

18A is situated right by a popular, unsheltered bus stop. Commuters would be able to browse and get inspired whilst they wait for their carriage to arrive.

There is a lot of warmth for this building on community groups, and it would be fantastic to have this restored as a space in which everyone is welcome. I love the poetry of this once being a staple of working-class communities, now in service to local working-class people.

Kennedys lots are dotted around South London. The restored branch I’m most familiar with is Crystal Eye Centre, an opticians situated on Crystal Palace’s ‘triangle’. They’ve done a fantastic job of restoring details alongside adding necessary functionality. This is best example of a repaired branch I am aware of.

Visual archive

I have a love for typography and I’ve documented signage, shopfronts, and wayfinding since my early twenties. South Norwood (and Croydon in general) has such a rich typographic history, and I’d love to surface this, making it available both online (via instagram and twitter), and printed to be displayed in the library. I hope this raises the esteem of South Norwood within design communities and help residents feel part of a great tradition.

Meeting space + gallery space

Communitea currently hold meetings at Costa Coffee. Whilst this is workable, a space free of commerce would be better suited to hold gatherings of this nature. A kettle and green teas would be enough to faciliate these meetings.

The library could serve as a place for artists to meet each other, develop their craft, and perhaps take the step of displaying their own work.

Everything I hope to achieve will be in collaboration with existing groups, and in dialogue with the community. This space could provide a crucial role in building awareness of groups and projects, and events, signposting and creating promotional material where necessary.

About me

I was born in 1983 at Mayday Hospital and raised in South Norwood, spending most of my life here, and returning in 2020. I think everyone’s worlds got smaller during the pandemic and walking the same streets I grew up in made me reflect on what community means to me. My vision for South Norwood is of a high street people are proud of, feel part of, and take care of. A beautiful, urban market-town. I am biased, but there’s no place else like South Norwood.
